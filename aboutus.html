A little History

  • 1960

    Bloodless Surgery

    Dr. Denton Cooley: "bloodless surgery" for Jehovah's Witness patients, a 3-step approach: optimization of red blood cell mass before surgery, minimizing blood loss, and tolerating postoperative anemia. Cooley reported on more than 12,000 transfusion-free surgeries.

  • 1970

    Blood Management

    The more inclusive term "blood management" refers to the supply of blood from altruistic donors.

  • 2005

    Focusing on the Patients

    It was first introduced by J.P. Isbister, an Australian hematologist who suggested that the focus of transfusion medicine should shift from concentrating on blood components to focusing on the patients.

  • 2010

    Recognizing Alternative

    The WHO recognized PBM to promote the availability of transfusion alternatives.

  • 2021

    Globally Standarizing PBM

    The WHO called for the implementation of PBM as a global standard of care, essentially the urgent need to implement PBM.

  • 2022

    New Standards Focusing on the Patient

    A new standard of patient care, given the clinical and economic impact it offers.
